NAGALAND



Cultural Identity and Attire Visual Identity: Traditional dress reflects a weaver's specific tribe, social standing, and historic achievements. Handwoven Shawls: Key identity markers include distinct handwoven shawls like the Tsungkotepsu and Changsü. Signature Jewelry Materials Used: Ornaments are meticulously handcrafted from glass beads, polished bovine bones, brass discs, boar tusks, and imported cowrie shells. Tribal Symbolism The Hornbill: The Great Indian Hornbill is Nagaland's ultimate tribal symbol, representing alertness, grandeur, and deep reverence. Ceremonial Headgear: Iconic black-and-white hornbill feathers are fastened to warrior headgear to dictate a family's prestige. Storytelling: Every individual ornament and woven line narrates a story of valor, community, and ancestral pride.
